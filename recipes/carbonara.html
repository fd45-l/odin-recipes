Description

Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish made with a rich, silky sauce of egg yolks, pecorino cheese, crispy guanciale,
and freshly ground black pepper. It's a simple yet deeply flavorful recipe that highlights traditional Italian ingredients without the use of cream or extras.

Ingredients

  • Pasta of choice
  • Pecorino cheese
  • Guanciale
  • Egg yolks
  • Black pepper
  1. Cook the pasta - Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente. Save some pasta water.
  2. Prepare the guanciale - Cut into small strips and cook in a pan until crispy.
  3. Make the sauce - In a bowl, mix egg yolks with grated pecorino and black pepper.
  4. Combine - Add drained pasta to the pan with guanciale (off heat), then quickly mix in the egg mixture, stirring fast.
  5. Adjust if needed - Add a bit of pasta water to create a creamy sauce. Serve immediately with more pecorino and pepper.
